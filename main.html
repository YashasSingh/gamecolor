Domain 1: Identity

The Art of Perseverance | Dark Souls

Axiom

Failure is not a defeat; it is a data point.

In Dark Souls, death is not a punishment but a teacher. Each failure reveals a mistake, a pattern, or a timing issue that can be corrected. This philosophy mirrors how I approach my own identity. Obstacles are signals to adapt, not proof of inadequacy.

Icon

The Bonfire represents stability in chaos, a place to reflect and prepare before reentering challenges.

“Don’t you dare go hollow.” — Laurentius of the Great Swamp

To go hollow is to lose purpose. Perseverance is a moral choice, choosing meaning over despair even when progress feels impossible.

Artifact

The Estus Flask symbolizes limited but renewable sources of strength: rest, support, and balance.

Domain 2: Family

The Bonds of Choice | Uncharted

Family is defined not only by blood, but by loyalty under pressure. The relationship between Nathan Drake and Victor Sullivan demonstrates how commitment persists through danger and uncertainty.

Icon

Sir Francis Drake’s Ring, engraved with Sic Parvis Magna, represents greatness shaped by guidance and trust.

“I’m not losing you because of some treasure. I’ve been looking out for you since you were a kid… and I’m not stopping now.” — Victor Sullivan

This reflects the mentors and family figures in my life who value character over achievement and integrity over success.

Domain 3: Politics and Citizenship

The Cost of Unregulated Power | Cyberpunk 2077

Cyberpunk 2077 presents a society where corporations eclipse governments and people become assets. Night City is a warning about profit without accountability.

Icon

The crossed-out Arasaka logo symbolizes rejection of a system that monetizes human value.

“The City of Dreams. I’m a big dreamer… but the city always wins.” — V

Citizenship requires vigilance and participation. Without regulation and resistance, power consumes the people it claims to serve.

Domain 4: World, Universe, and Nature

The Hubris of Creation | Detroit: Become Human

Detroit: Become Human questions the assumption that creation grants ownership. When sentient systems are created, responsibility follows. Innovation without accountability is arrogance.

Icon

The blue LED circle represents the fragile boundary between object and life.

“An eye for an eye and the world goes blind… but what do you do when they take both your eyes?” — Markus

This question exposes the consequences of exploitation. When humanity treats the world as a tool rather than a partner, systems collapse.

Artifact

The Jericho graffiti tag symbolizes suppressed consciousness inevitably rising. Harmony comes from coexistence, not domination.

Identity is built through choice, experience, and perseverance.
